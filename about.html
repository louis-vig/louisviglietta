About me

I am a student in the Computer Science department at Princeton University. I have a passion for technology, and love learning and applying new skills to create exciting projects, whether for fun or to solve critical problems! When not in class, I play flute for the Princeton University Sinfonia and serve as the treasurer of First College Council.

Experience

I have done lab-based research in areas ranging from superconducting crystals, chemotherapeutic drug mechanisms, and computational metabolic engineering. During my most recent research experience, I helped identify candidate genetic modifications in the yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae that could potentially increase their production rates of ethyl acetate. I achieved this by using genome scale models of yeast metabolism and COBRA (constraint based reconstruction and analysis) to simulate in MATLAB how these modified strains would behave in different growth conditions. By editing existing models and adding heterologous pathways, I helped to identify candidate yeast strains with increased production capacity and even the ability to uptake additional substrates. If confirmed in vivo, this could make bioproduction of ethyl acetate more feasible, which could eliminate or reduce the need for petroleum-based production.
